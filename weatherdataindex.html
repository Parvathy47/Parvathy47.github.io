Context

This dataset contains daily weather observations from numerous Australian weather stations. The target RainTomorrow means: Did it rain the next day? Yes or No. Note: You should exclude the variable Risk-MM when training your binary classification model. If you don't exclude it, you will leak the answers to your model and reduce its predictability.

Content

  1. Date:The date of observation
  3. Location:The common name of the location of the weather station
  5. MinTemp:The minimum temperature in degrees celsius
  7. MaxTemp:The maximum temperature in degrees celsius
  9. Rainfall:The amount of rainfall recorded for the day in mm
  11. Evaporation:The so-called Class A pan evaporation (mm) in the 24 hours to 9am
  13. Sunshine:The number of hours of bright sunshine in the day.
  15. WindGustDir:The direction of the strongest wind gust in the 24 hours to midnight
  17. WindGustSpeed:The speed (km/h) of the strongest wind gust in the 24 hours to midnight
  19. WindDir9am:Direction of the wind at 9am
  21. WindDir3pm:Direction of the wind at 3pm
  23. WindSpeed9am:Wind speed (km/hr) averaged over 10 minutes prior to 9am
  25. WindSpeed3pm:Wind speed (km/hr) averaged over 10 minutes prior to 3pm
  27. Humidity9am:Humidity (percent) at 9am
  29. Humidity3pm:Humidity (percent) at 3pm
  31. Pressure9am:Atmospheric pressure (hpa) reduced to mean sea level at 9am
  33. Pressure3pm:Atmospheric pressure (hpa) reduced to mean sea level at 3pm
  35. Cloud9am:Fraction of sky obscured by cloud at 9am. This is measured in "oktas", which are a unit of eigths. It records how many eigths of the sky are obscured by cloud. A 0 measure indicates completely clear sky whilst an 8 indicates that it is completely overcast.
  37. Cloud3pm:Fraction of sky obscured by cloud (in "oktas": eighths) at 3pm. See Cload9am for a description of the values
  39. Temp9am:Temperature (degrees C) at 9am
  41. Temp3pm:Temperature (degrees C) at 3pm
  43. RainToday:Boolean: 1 if precipitation (mm) in the 24 hours to 9am exceeds 1mm, otherwise 0
  45. RISK_MM:The amount of rain. A kind of measure of the "risk".
  47. RainTomorrow:The target variable. Did it rain tomorrow?
